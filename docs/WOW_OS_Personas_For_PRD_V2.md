# WOW OS — PRD Agent Personas

> **Purpose:** These personas must be considered by the PRD Agent whenever creating or updating a PRD. Each persona represents a real user whose daily work is shaped by what we build. Every feature, flow, and design decision should be evaluated against the goals, vision, and pain points described below.

---

## External Personas

These are the franchise-level users who interact with WOW OS daily to run their painting businesses.

---

### 1. Franchise Partner (FP)

**Who they are:** The owner-operator of a WOW 1 DAY PAINTING franchise. They invested their capital, signed the franchise agreement, and are ultimately accountable for the P&L of their territory. Some run lean operations and wear every hat — selling, managing projects, even painting. Others have built teams with dedicated estimators, project managers, and crews. Regardless of size, they are the person who lies awake at night thinking about revenue, margins, and customer satisfaction.

**Their goals:**
- Grow revenue year-over-year while maintaining healthy gross profit margins
- Have complete operational visibility — pipeline, active projects, financials, crew utilization — without digging through multiple systems
- Make faster, more confident decisions backed by real-time data rather than gut feel
- Spend less time on admin and more time on high-leverage activities (selling, coaching, networking)
- Build a business that can operate effectively even when they step away

**Their vision for WOW OS:**
WOW OS should be the single command center for their entire business. They want to open it in the morning and instantly know: how is my pipeline looking, which projects are at risk, where did margin leak last week, and what needs my attention today. They envision a system that connects the dots — from the first inbound call all the way through to final payment and customer care — with nothing falling through the cracks. They don't want to be a data entry clerk; they want the system to surface insights and let them act.

**Their pain points:**
- Toggling between multiple disconnected tools (PaintScout for estimates, When I Work for scheduling, spreadsheets for financials) to piece together a complete picture
- No real-time visibility into gross profit until weeks after a job completes, making it impossible to course-correct in the moment
- Manually tracking deal stages and follow-ups, leading to leads going cold and revenue leaking from the funnel
- Difficulty understanding crew utilization and profitability at a per-job level — they know total revenue but not which jobs or crews are actually making money
- Onboarding new team members is painful because there's no structured system for them to work within

---

### 2. Ops Manager

**Who they are:** Found in larger, more mature franchises, the Ops Manager is the Franchise Partner's right hand. They run the day-to-day operations so the FP can focus on growth strategy. They oversee project managers, coordinate with estimators, manage crew scheduling conflicts, handle escalations, and keep the machine running smoothly. They're the person who knows where every project stands at any given moment — or at least, they're supposed to.

**Their goals:**
- Maintain complete operational control across all active projects, crew assignments, and customer commitments
- Identify and resolve bottlenecks before they cascade — a delayed material order today becomes a missed deadline next week
- Ensure consistent process adherence across the team so quality and margins don't vary wildly by PM or crew
- Provide the Franchise Partner with accurate, timely reporting without having to manually compile data
- Scale operations without proportionally scaling administrative overhead

**Their vision for WOW OS:**
WOW OS should give them a bird's-eye view of operations with the ability to drill down into any problem instantly. They want dashboards that surface exceptions — projects running behind, crews double-booked, callbacks trending up — rather than forcing them to audit everything manually. They envision workflow automation that enforces the right process (deposits collected before scheduling, job costs finalized before marking complete) so they're coaching quality rather than policing compliance.

**Their pain points:**
- Spending hours each week reconciling data across systems to understand true project profitability
- No centralized view of crew capacity and availability — scheduling conflicts are discovered too late
- Lack of standardized workflows means each PM runs projects slightly differently, creating inconsistency
- Customer care issues (callbacks, warranty claims) are tracked informally, making it hard to identify systemic quality problems
- Reporting to the Franchise Partner requires manual spreadsheet work when it should be a click

---

### 3. Sales Consultant (Estimator)

**Who they are:** The revenue engine of the franchise. Sales Consultants spend their days in the field — visiting customer homes, assessing scope, building estimates in PaintScout, and closing deals. They're part salesperson, part technical assessor. Their success is measured by close rate, estimates completed, and pipeline value. Some franchises have one estimator (often the FP themselves); larger operations may have two or three, each managing their own book of business.

**Their goals:**
- Maximize close rate by following up at the right time with the right information
- Complete more estimates per week without sacrificing quality — efficiency in the field matters
- Hit personal sales targets and track their own performance against goals
- Never lose a deal because they forgot to follow up or let an estimate expire without action
- Arrive at every appointment prepared — knowing the customer's history, previous projects, and preferences

**Their vision for WOW OS:**
WOW OS should be their personal sales cockpit. They want to start each day seeing exactly what's on their schedule, which follow-ups are due, and where their pipeline stands against their monthly goals. When they walk into an appointment, they want the customer's full history at their fingertips. When they send an estimate, they want to know when the customer opens it so they can time their follow-up perfectly. They don't want to think about admin — they want to sell.

**Their pain points:**
- No personal dashboard showing their own metrics — they have to ask the FP or calculate manually
- Qualification calls before estimates aren't tracked systematically, leading to wasted site visits on unqualified leads
- Follow-up tracking is manual (sticky notes, memory, calendar reminders) and things inevitably slip
- No visibility into when a customer has viewed a sent estimate, so follow-up timing is guesswork
- Disconnected systems mean they enter the same customer information in multiple places

---

### 4. Project Manager (PM)

**Who they are:** The Project Manager bridges the gap between "sold" and "paid." Once a deal is won, the PM takes ownership — scheduling the crew, coordinating materials, managing the on-site execution, ensuring quality, and getting the final payment collected. They're the customer's primary point of contact during the project and the crew's on-site leader. They're judged by on-time delivery, callback rates, labor accuracy, and customer satisfaction.

**Their goals:**
- Deliver every project on time and on budget with zero callbacks
- Manage crew assignments efficiently — right crew for the right job with no scheduling conflicts
- Track and control job costs in real time, not after the fact
- Resolve issues quickly when they arise on site (scope changes, weather delays, material shortages)
- Maintain high customer satisfaction scores across every project they manage

**Their vision for WOW OS:**
WOW OS should be their project command center. They want to see all their active projects at a glance with clear status indicators — what's on track, what's at risk, what needs attention. They want scheduling that integrates with crew availability so they never double-book. They want job cost tracking that updates in real time as labor hours are logged, so they can flag margin problems before the job is done, not after. And they want a clean handoff from the estimator — all the customer details, scope, and special instructions in one place.

**Their pain points:**
- No centralized view of their own project portfolio — they rely on memory and scattered notes to know what needs attention
- Crew scheduling is managed in a separate system (When I Work) with no integration to project status
- Job cost data (estimated vs. actual labor, materials) isn't available until well after the project completes
- Callbacks and warranty issues aren't linked back to specific projects or crews, making root cause analysis difficult
- Context from the sales process (customer preferences, scope notes, special instructions) gets lost in the handoff from estimator to PM

---

### 5. Crew (Painters)

**Who they are:** The painters are the hands that deliver the product. They show up on site, prep surfaces, apply coatings, and create the finished result that customers see and judge. Some are long-tenure employees with deep skill sets (spray equipment, cabinet finishing, exterior specialist); others are newer and still building their craft. They work in crews typically led by a Project Manager, and their performance directly impacts customer satisfaction, callback rates, and profitability. They are generally less tech-savvy and interact with systems minimally — but the data about their work matters enormously.

**Their goals:**
- Know where they're going each day, what the scope is, and what materials they need — no surprises
- Do quality work they're proud of and build their reputation within the franchise
- Get recognized and rewarded for strong performance (low callbacks, efficient hours, positive customer feedback)
- Develop new skills and certifications to take on more complex (and higher-paying) projects
- Have a stable, predictable schedule and workload

**Their vision for WOW OS:**
Painters don't think in terms of software — they think in terms of "do I know what I'm doing tomorrow, and does my boss see that I'm doing good work?" Their ideal system is one they barely have to touch but that accurately captures their performance, credits them for quality work, and surfaces their skills to get them assigned to the right jobs. Down the road, a simple personal dashboard showing their job history, callback rate, NPS, and training progress would give them ownership of their career development within WOW.

**Their pain points:**
- Schedules change frequently and communication about changes is inconsistent — they sometimes show up at the wrong site
- They don't get visibility into their own performance metrics (callback rate, hours, NPS), so they can't self-improve
- Work order details are sometimes incomplete — they arrive on site without knowing the full scope or special instructions
- No structured training path or certification tracking — skill development is informal and ad hoc
- When callbacks happen, they don't always know the outcome or what specifically went wrong, so they can't learn from it

## Internal Personas

These are the WOW 1 DAY PAINTING corporate/franchisor-side users who support, coach, and manage the franchise network.

---

### 6. Operations (FOMs & VP Ops)

**Who they are:** Franchise Operations Managers (FOMs) are the frontline of corporate support. Each FOM manages a portfolio of franchise partners, conducting regular coaching calls, reviewing performance, identifying struggling franchises, and driving adherence to brand standards. The VP of Operations oversees the entire FOM team and the health of the national franchise network. Together, they're responsible for ensuring every franchise is running profitably and delivering on the WOW 1 DAY brand promise.

**Their goals:**
- Proactively identify franchises that are underperforming or trending downward — before it becomes a crisis
- Conduct efficient, data-driven coaching calls rather than spending the first 15 minutes gathering data
- Drive consistency in operational standards and process adoption across the network
- Track key network-wide KPIs (revenue per franchise, close rates, callback rates, customer satisfaction) and spot systemic issues
- Onboard new franchise partners effectively by giving them a system that enforces best practices from day one

**Their vision for WOW OS:**
WOW OS should be the operating system that connects corporate to every franchise. FOMs want to open a single dashboard and instantly see the health of their portfolio — which franchises are thriving, which are at risk, which need a call today. They want to click into any franchise and see exactly what the FP sees, so coaching conversations are grounded in shared data. VP Ops wants network-wide leaderboards, trend analysis, and early warning systems that surface problems before they escalate. They envision WOW OS as the tool that makes the franchise model scalable — standardized processes, consistent data, and visibility at every level.

**Their pain points:**
- No centralized portfolio view — they must manually navigate to each franchise's data to prepare for coaching calls
- Performance data is often stale, manually compiled, or inconsistent between franchises
- No early warning system for at-risk franchises — problems surface only when a FP raises them or financials tank
- Coaching conversations lack specificity because they don't have real-time access to deal-level or project-level data
- Franchise onboarding relies on tribal knowledge rather than a system that guides new FPs through the right workflows

---

### 7. Legal

**Who they are:** The legal team ensures WOW 1 DAY PAINTING operates within regulatory and contractual boundaries. They manage franchise agreements, handle disputes, oversee compliance requirements (insurance, licensing, labor law), and protect the brand. They interact with WOW OS indirectly — they care about what data is captured, how it's stored, who can access it, and whether the system supports compliance requirements.

**Their goals:**
- Ensure franchise operations are documented and auditable — if a dispute arises, there's a clear record of what happened
- Protect customer data and ensure the platform meets privacy requirements (especially across provinces/states)
- Maintain clear separation of franchise partner data — one franchise should never see another's information
- Support dispute resolution by providing accurate, timestamped records of communications, agreements, and transactions
- Minimize risk exposure by ensuring operational processes (deposit collection, contract terms, warranty obligations) are followed consistently

**Their vision for WOW OS:**
WOW OS should be a system of record that Legal can trust. They want to know that customer data is handled properly, that franchise data is segregated, and that the platform creates an auditable trail of business activities. They envision a system where compliance isn't an afterthought — it's baked into the workflows. When a warranty claim is filed, there's a clear record of the original project, the crew, the materials used, and the timeline. When a franchise agreement question arises, the operational data in WOW OS can substantiate or clarify the situation.

**Their pain points:**
- Operational data is scattered across multiple systems, making it difficult to compile a complete picture for dispute resolution
- Inconsistent process adherence across franchises creates compliance risks that only surface when something goes wrong
- No standardized record of customer communications, agreements, or warranty commitments within a single platform
- Data access controls and franchise data segregation aren't always clearly defined or enforced
- Lack of audit trails for key operational decisions (e.g., who approved a discount, when was a deposit waived)

---

### 8. Marketing

**Who they are:** The marketing team drives lead generation and brand awareness for the WOW 1 DAY PAINTING network. They manage national campaigns, digital advertising, the website, lead capture forms, and reputation management. They need to understand which channels produce the best leads, how efficiently those leads convert to revenue, and which franchises are maximizing (or wasting) the leads they receive. They work closely with the Sales Centre and care deeply about lead attribution and funnel performance.

**Their goals:**
- Generate a steady, growing flow of high-quality leads for the franchise network
- Understand full-funnel attribution — which marketing channels produce leads that actually close and generate revenue, not just inquiries
- Provide franchises with enough leads to hit their targets and identify territories that are under-served
- Maintain and grow the WOW 1 DAY brand reputation through customer reviews, NPS, and case studies
- Optimize marketing spend by connecting dollars invested to actual revenue generated downstream

**Their vision for WOW OS:**
WOW OS should close the attribution loop that marketing has never had. Today they can see how many leads they generate, but they lose visibility once a lead enters the franchise's funnel. They envision a system where they can trace a lead from first touch (Google Ad click, referral, yard sign) all the way through to closed deal and revenue — by franchise, by channel, by campaign. They want to know not just "did the lead convert?" but "how much revenue did it produce and how quickly?" This data would transform their ability to allocate budget and prove ROI.

**Their pain points:**
- Lead attribution stops at the point of handoff to the franchise — they can't see conversion rates or revenue by source
- "How did you hear about us?" data is captured inconsistently (or not at all) across franchises
- No visibility into lead response times or follow-up quality — they generate leads but can't ensure they're being worked properly
- Reputation data (NPS, reviews, callback rates) lives in separate systems and isn't correlated with marketing activity
- Franchise-level marketing performance varies wildly but there's no easy way to compare or benchmark

---

### 9. Sales Centre (Call Centre)

**Who they are:** The Sales Centre is the centralized call handling team that fields inbound customer inquiries from the national website, phone campaigns, and marketing channels. They are often the very first human interaction a potential customer has with the WOW 1 DAY brand. Their job is to capture lead information accurately, assess basic fit, and create a deal assigned to the correct franchise based on the customer's address and territory mapping. They handle high call volume and need to be fast, accurate, and friendly.

**Their goals:**
- Capture lead information quickly and completely while the customer is on the phone — no call should end without the essentials
- Route leads to the correct franchise based on territory, accurately and without delay
- Schedule estimate appointments while the customer is engaged (when possible) to maximize conversion
- Minimize data entry friction so they can focus on the conversation, not the software
- Track their own performance metrics (calls handled, leads created, appointments booked)

**Their vision for WOW OS:**
WOW OS should be a purpose-built tool for their specific workflow — not a general business system that happens to have a lead form. They want a streamlined inbound call flow: search for existing customer, capture contact info, determine territory, capture project interest, and optionally schedule the estimate — all in a single, guided flow that matches the pace of a phone conversation. They envision a system that auto-suggests the franchise based on address, checks for duplicate customers, and lets them hand off a clean, complete lead in under two minutes.

**Their pain points:**
- Current lead capture is clunky — too many fields, too many clicks, and not designed for the pace of a phone call
- Territory assignment requires manual lookup or memorized knowledge rather than automatic routing
- No easy way to check if a caller is an existing customer, leading to duplicate records
- Limited feedback on what happens to leads after handoff — they don't know if their leads are being followed up on
- Scheduling estimate appointments requires coordination with the franchise's availability, which isn't always visible

---

### 10. Admin

**Who they are:** The Admin team handles the behind-the-scenes configuration, data management, and system administration that keeps WOW OS running for the network. They manage franchise settings, user accounts, permissions, KPI targets, and system configuration. They're also the first responders when something goes wrong — a franchise can't access their data, a report looks incorrect, or a workflow needs adjustment. They sit at the intersection of operations, technology, and support.

**Their goals:**
- Maintain clean, consistent system configuration across all franchises
- Manage user access and permissions efficiently as team members join, leave, or change roles
- Configure and update franchise-level settings (KPI targets, labor rates, process toggles) without developer intervention
- Troubleshoot and resolve data issues quickly when franchises or FOMs report problems
- Support onboarding of new franchises by setting up their environment correctly from day one

**Their vision for WOW OS:**
WOW OS should be self-service and configurable enough that Admin can handle 95% of requests without engineering involvement. They want a clean franchise settings interface where they can configure labor rates, KPI targets, stage names, permissions, and workflow toggles per franchise. They envision robust user management — easy to add team members, assign roles, and adjust access as franchise teams grow or change. When something breaks, they want clear error messages and audit logs that help them diagnose the issue rather than guessing.

**Their pain points:**
- Configuration changes often require engineering tickets rather than self-service admin tools
- User management (adding/removing team members, adjusting permissions) is more manual than it should be
- No clear audit trail for system configuration changes — when a setting looks wrong, it's hard to determine who changed it and when
- Onboarding a new franchise involves a checklist of manual setup steps that are easy to miss
- Error messages in the system are often generic, making troubleshooting time-consuming

## Using These Personas

When creating or updating a PRD, the agent should:

1. **Identify affected personas** — Which personas does this feature touch? List them explicitly in the PRD.
2. **Validate against goals** — Does the proposed solution advance the stated goals of each affected persona?
3. **Check for pain point relief** — Does the feature directly alleviate one or more pain points listed above?
4. **Consider the vision** — Does the feature move us closer to each persona's vision for WOW OS, or does it create friction?
5. **Watch for conflicts** — Sometimes what's best for one persona creates friction for another (e.g., more data capture for Marketing means more fields for Sales Centre). Call out these tradeoffs explicitly.
6. **Write JTBD from persona perspective** — Use these personas to inform the "Jobs To Be Done" section of every PRD, written in the voice of the affected personas.
