## Design Context

### Users
- **Franchise Partners**: Individual WOW 1 DAY PAINTING franchise owners checking their own cash position, typically during their weekly ritual at a desk. Need clarity on where they stand financially without needing to be a finance expert.
- **Franchise Operations Managers (FOMs)**: Overseeing multiple franchises, reviewing and comparing cash health across locations. Need to quickly spot problems and drill into specifics.
- Both are primary audiences with equal importance. The interface must serve the solo owner reviewing one franchise as well as the FOM scanning across many.

### Brand Personality
**Calm, trustworthy, composed** — like a reliable financial advisor. No drama, no flash. Confidence through clarity.

Three words: **Steady. Clear. Capable.**

Emotional goal: Users should feel *in control* of their cash flow, not anxious about it. The interface should reduce financial stress, not amplify it.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Warm neutrals, natural greens (brand: #8BC34A), light backgrounds. The existing palette leans into muted earth-greens and soft grays — lean into this rather than fighting it.
- **Theme**: Light mode. The tan (#f7f6f3) and blue-gray (#f4f6f9) backgrounds already create a calm, papery quality.
- **Typography**: DM Sans — clean, friendly, slightly geometric. Works well for the approachable-yet-professional tone.
- **Anti-references**: Generic SaaS dashboards — cookie-cutter card grids, identical metric boxes, interchangeable B2B tool aesthetic. This should feel *specific* to franchise cash flow management, not like a template.
- **Sidebar**: Dark navy (#1e2a47) sidebar provides grounding contrast against the light content area.

### Design Principles
1. **Clarity over cleverness**: Every element should communicate, not decorate. If it doesn't help the user understand their cash position, it doesn't belong.
2. **Hierarchy through restraint**: Use size, weight, and spacing to guide the eye — not color splashes or card borders. The most important number should be obvious without any labels.
3. **Consistency breeds trust**: Identical patterns for identical actions. If "Add" works one way on one page, it works the same way everywhere. Financial tools earn trust through predictability.
4. **Calm density**: Show useful information without overwhelm. Tables and data views should feel organized and scannable, not sparse or cluttered.
5. **Earned color**: Color is reserved for meaning — health status (green/amber/red), categories, and primary actions. Decorative color is avoided.
